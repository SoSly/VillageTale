Transform Minecraft villagers from emerald vending machines into actual workers with needs, schedules, and the ability to craft items using real materials.

## Vision

Inspired by TekTopia, VillageWorks reimagines villagers as living beings who wake up hungry, work with tools that break, take breaks when tired, and actually craft items instead of magically trading them. Build a village and watch it come alive as villagers go about their daily lives—no more standing at workstations waiting for trades!

## Getting Started

1. Build a Town Hall to establish your village
2. Craft a Mayor's Ledger to manage your village
3. Use the ledger to hire vanilla villagers into your workforce
4. Designate work areas, homes, and storage
5. Watch your village come to life!

## Core Features

### Village Management
- **Mayor's Ledger:** Your primary tool for managing the village—designate work areas, assign homes, set production limits
- **Hiring System:** Convert vanilla villagers into productive members of your village
- **Town Hall:** The heart of your village where traveling villagers arrive with quests and trade opportunities
- **Work Areas:** Define specific zones where villagers perform their jobs
- **Shared Storage:** Set up communal storage that villagers contribute to and draw from
- **Production Limits:** Control how much of each item crafters and gatherers maintain in storage
- **Path Networks:** Villagers move faster on path, stone, or wood blocks—design efficient village layouts!

### Living Villagers
- **Hunger System:** Villagers need food to work and consume hunger when performing tasks
- **Daily Schedules:** Wake up, eat breakfast, work, take breaks, socialize in the evening, sleep
- **Personal Inventory:** Each villager manages their own tools and supplies
- **Assigned Beds:** Every villager has their own bed they return to each night
- **Aging:** Villagers progress from children to adults to eventually dying of old age

### Work & Crafting
- **Real Crafting:** No more magic trades—villagers craft items using actual materials from storage
- **Tool Usage:** Villagers use and wear out tools just like players do
- **Recipe Learning:** Teach crafters new recipes (limited by their Intelligence stat)
- **Continuous Production:** Villagers automatically craft items up to configured limits
- **Resource Management:** Smart villagers pull materials from and contribute to shared storage

### Villager Stats
Stats affect how well villagers perform their duties:
- **Strength:** Carrying capacity, mining speed, tree-chopping speed, and weapon damage
- **Endurance:** Work speed, break frequency, and hunger consumption rate
- **Intelligence:** Recipe capacity and crafting success rate (low Intelligence means wasted materials!)

### Social Life & Families

#### Relationships
- **Organic Friendships:** Villagers who interact frequently develop relationships
- **Romance:** Some friendships blossom into romantic partnerships
- **Marriage:** Couples in love request adjoining beds to move in together
- **Family Growth:** Married villagers eventually request nursery space for children

#### Children & Development
- **Learning Through Play:** Children develop stats based on their activities:
 —Playing tag builds Endurance
 —Climbing trees builds Strength  
 —Hide and seek builds Intelligence
- **Apprenticeship:** Children can watch adults work, learning recipes and helping with simple tasks
- **Natural Growth:** Every few minutes, children choose new activities, organically developing different skills
- **Life Cycle:** Children grow into productive adults who eventually seek their own homes

### Happiness System
A happy village is a productive village:
- **Housing Quality:** Adequate living space keeps villagers content
- **Social Spaces:** Taverns and gathering areas for evening socializing
- **Village Atmosphere:** Decorations, flowers, and lighting improve mood
- **Population Balance:** Overcrowding causes stress and unhappiness
- **Grief:** Recent deaths affect the entire village, especially close friends
- **Cemetery:** Proper burial ceremonies reduce happiness loss from deaths
- **Effects:** Happiness impacts work efficiency, movement speed, and family planning

### Quest System
- **Personal Requests:** Villagers communicate their needs—new beds, better housing, job opportunities
- **Traveling Merchants:** Visitors bring requests from distant villages with unique rewards
- **Village Projects:** Villagers suggest improvements like taverns, decorations, or infrastructure

## Professions

### Currently Planned
- **Guards:** Patrol the village and defend against threats
- **Miners:** Dig shafts and extract underground resources
- **Lumberjacks:** Manage tree farms and provide wood
- **Herdsmen:** Tend to and breed livestock
- **Farmers:** Grow crops and manage fields (enhancing vanilla farmers)
- **Blacksmiths:** Craft tools, weapons, and armor (enhancing vanilla toolsmiths/weaponsmiths/armorers)
- **Children:** Not a profession, but a life stage focused on play and learning

### Integration with Vanilla
We're reimagining vanilla professions to fit the VillageWorks system—farmers will actually farm, blacksmiths will actually forge, and so on. More details coming as development progresses!
